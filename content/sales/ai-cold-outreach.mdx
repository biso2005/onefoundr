## The Cold Outreach Reality (And Why It's Broken)

"Hi [First Name], I noticed you're in [Industry]. We help [companies like you] achieve [vague benefit]. Want to chat?"

The catch: personalizing 100 emails takes 20+ hours. That's not scalable for a solopreneur.

Enter AI. Now you can personalize 100 emails in 2 hours.

## The 5-Step Cold Outreach System

### Step 1: Build Your Prospect List

**Time:** 1-2 hours for 50 prospects

Do this manually using LinkedIn Sales Navigator or Apollo:
1. Filter by target company size, role, industry
2. Save the list (name, company, title, LinkedIn URL)
3. Get email address (Hunter.io has a free plugin)
4. Throw into spreadsheet

You now have 50 people who fit your ideal customer profile.

### Step 2: Research Each Prospect

**Time:** 2-3 minutes per person with AI (about 2-3 hours for 50)

For each prospect:
1. Go to their LinkedIn profile
2. Read their last 5-10 posts
3. Look for recent company announcements
4. Ask Perplexity: "What are common challenges for [role] at [company size] that work in [industry]?"
5. Identify 1-2 specific problems they likely have

**Pro tip:** Record this in a spreadsheet column so you can reference it when writing.

Example:
| Name | Company | Role | Signal | Problem They Likely Have |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sarah | TechCorp | VP Marketing | Just hired new team | Needs to establish new marketing ops |
| Mike | StartupX | CEO | Announced Series A | Scaling support team, hiring pain |

### Step 3: AI Drafts Personalized Email

**Time:** 5 minutes per 5 people with AI

You're going to create an AI prompt that personalizes at scale.

**Here's the prompt template:**

```
You are a [YOUR ROLE] who helps [YOUR TARGET CUSTOMER] solve [YOUR CORE PROBLEM].

Write a cold email to [PROSPECT NAME] at [COMPANY].

Key information:
- Their role: [ROLE]
- Their company: [COMPANY]
- Recent signal: [WHY YOU'RE REACHING OUT - e.g., "just hired marketing team"]
- Their likely problem: [PROBLEM]
- Your solution: [WHAT YOU DO]

Your email should:
1. Reference the specific signal (show you did research)
2. Name their specific problem (show you understand their situation)
3. Mention how you've solved this for similar companies
4. End with a specific, small ask (not "want to chat?" but "want to see how this worked for [similar company]?")
5. Be 4-6 sentences max
6. Sound like a real person, not a template

Write the email now.
```

**What Claude outputs:**
A customized email that references their specific situation, shows research, and feels personal.

**Example output:**
Hi Sarah,

I noticed TechCorp just brought on a new marketing team—congrats on the expansion. the thing I hear from VPs in your situation is that assembling the ops stack usually takes 2-3 months when it could happen in two weeks.

We help teams like yours compress their ops onboarding timeline using frameworks that work with new hires instead of against them. Helped [Another Company] go from zero to operational in 14 days.

Would it be worth 15min to see how we structured that?

—[Your name]
```


Notice: It references her recent news (new team), names her specific pain (assembling ops takes time), shows social proof (another company did it in 14 days), and makes a small ask (15min call).

This is not a generic template. Prospect will read it.

### Step 4: You Review and Customize (If Needed)

**Time:** 1-2 minutes per email

Read each AI draft:
- Does it feel authentic to your voice?
- Is the problem accurate?
- Is the ask reasonable?
- Are there typos or weird phrasing?

If yes to most: hit send.
If no: edit or ask Claude to rewrite.

Tweak 1-2 things, then ship.

### Step 5: Track and Follow Up

**Time:** 5 minutes daily

1. Check who opened the email (via email platform)
2. Check who clicked the link (if you included one)
3. Check who replied

**Follow-up timing:**
- No response in 3 days: send follow-up #1 (reference original email, add new insight)
- No response to #1 in 4 days: send follow-up #2 (different angle, softer ask)
- No response to #2 in 5 days: stop (move to next prospect)

**Follow-up template (also AI-drafted):**
Hi Sarah,

Quick follow-up on my previous note—meant to mention: [NEW INSIGHT relevant to their situation].

Most teams we work with see the structure I mentioned reduce ops setup time by 60-70%.

If you see value in that, open to chatting. If not, no worries.

—[Your name]
```


Notice: It gives new information (not just "did you see my email?"), shows specific benefit, and makes the no-response option clear (reduces pressure).

## Common Mistakes (And How to Prevent Them)

**Mistake 1: Sounding like AI**
- You use the exact Claude output without editing
- Prospect reads it and thinks "yeah, this was written by an algorithm"
- Response rate drops

**Better:** Edit 1-2 sentences to match your voice. Add a line that only you would write. Real people have quirks; AI doesn't.

**Mistake 2: Not personalizing enough**
- You use the AI framework but don't research the specific prospect
- Email says "I noticed you work in [industry]" (could apply to anyone)
- Doesn't feel personal

**Better:** Always research first. AI drafts based on your research, not instead of it.

**Mistake 3: Making the ask too big**
- "Want to grab a 30-min call to discuss?"
- Cold prospect doesn't owe you time
- They say no

**Better:** Ask for 15 minutes max. Or ask to send information. Make it low-friction.

**Mistake 4: Not following up**
- You send one email
- No response in 3 days
- You assume they're not interested
- You never follow up

**Better:** Follow up 2-3x. Most deals close on follow-up #2 or #3, not the first email.

**Mistake 5: Targeting wrong people**
- Your ideal customer is mid-market B2B
- You're reaching out to SMBs and enterprises
- No one's a fit

**Better:** Get specific about your ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) first. Build prospect list around that. Narrow > wide.
