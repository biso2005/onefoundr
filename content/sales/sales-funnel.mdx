# Sales Funnel for Solopreneurs: Simple Setup

A sales funnel is the journey a prospect takes from stranger to customer.

Most solopreneurs have no funnel. They hope people buy. They don't have a system.

A funnel is simple: Get attention → Build interest → Make offer → Close sale.

You don't need complicated. You need effective.

---

## The 3-Stage Solopreneur Funnel

### Stage 1: Awareness (Get Attention)

How do people find you?

**Free content:**
- Blog posts that rank on Google
- YouTube videos
- Podcast
- Twitter/LinkedIn posts
- Newsletter

**Referrals:**
- Word of mouth
- Guest appearances
- Collaborations
- Communities you participate in

**Paid ads** (optional):
- Google ads
- Facebook/LinkedIn ads
- YouTube ads

### Stage 2: Consideration (Build Interest)

They know you exist. Now do they want what you offer?

**Build credibility:**
- Show previous results
- Share case studies
- Post testimonials
- Demonstrate expertise

**Make it easy:**
- Clear website showing what you do
- About page telling your story
- Services page explaining offer
- FAQ addressing common concerns

**Nurture interest:**
- Email sequence after they opt-in
- Value-packed newsletter
- Free resources
- Conversations (reply to messages)

### Stage 3: Decision (Make Offer & Close)

They're interested. Now they decide.

**The offer:**
- Clear pricing
- Outcome-focused (not feature-focused)
- Limited options (don't overwhelm)
- Call-to-action (clear next step)

**The close:**
- Sales call if applicable
- Payment link
- Contract if needed
- Onboarding process

## The Minimal Funnel (Start Here)

You don't need everything. Start with the minimum:

### Asset 1: Lead Magnet
Free thing people get in exchange for email.

Examples:
- 30-item checklist
- "How to" guide
- Spreadsheet template
- Email sequence

### Asset 2: Welcome Email Sequence
5 emails that build trust and credibility.

Email 1: Deliver on lead magnet promise
Email 2: Ask about their problem
Email 3: Share your story
Email 4: Give free advice
Email 5: Introduce your offer

### Asset 3: Your Website/Landing Page
Shows:
- Who you help (headline)
- What you solve (subheading)
- How you solve it (your approach)
- Results (social proof, testimonials)
- Call to action (clear next step)

### Asset 4: Sales Page
If you have a paid offer:
- Headline addressing main pain
- Problem statement (they feel seen)
- Your solution
- What they get
- Who it's for (and who it's NOT for)
- Testimonials
- Price
- Clear CTA (buy now, schedule call)

## Simple Funnel Numbers

Assuming you get 1,000 website visitors per month:

```
1,000 visitors
↓ (2% signup for lead magnet)
20 leads
↓ (open welcome email sequence)
18 read emails
↓ (10% express interest)
2 interested
↓ (50% ready to buy)
1 new customer
```

At $500/month per customer:
- 1,000 visitors = $500/month recurring

Double your visitors, double your revenue.

## Email Sequence That Converts

### The 5-Email Welcome Sequence

**Email 1: Deliver + Delight**
- Subject: "Here's your [lead magnet]"
- Deliver promised resource
- Show enthusiasm about them joining
- No selling

**Email 2: Understand**
- Subject: "Quick question"
- Ask about their biggest challenge
- "Hit reply and let me know..."
- Build relationship

**Email 3: Trust**
- Subject: "Here's my story"
- Share your journey
- Why you understand their pain
- What you've learned
- Still no selling

**Email 4: Prove**
- Subject: "How I'd solve your problem"
- Give free advice on their challenge
- Share a framework or process
- Show your expertise
- Soft mention: "This is what I help people with"

**Email 5: Offer**
- Subject: "When you're ready..."
- Acknowledge they might want help
- Here's what you offer
- Here's how it works
- Link to more info or calendar
- Not pushy, just available

## Funnel Stages Explained

### Stage 1: Awareness (Traffic)

**Goal**: Get eyeballs on your content

**How**:
- Write SEO blog posts
- Post on social media
- Create videos
- Guest appearances
- Ads (if budget allows)

**Success metric**: 1,000+ monthly visitors

### Stage 2: Consideration (Building trust)

**Goal**: They know you, like you, sort of trust you

**How**:
- Lead magnet (shows competence)
- Email sequence (builds relationship)
- Case studies (proves you deliver)
- Testimonials (others vouch for you)
- Newsletter (stay visible)

**Success metric**: 20+ leads per 1,000 visitors (2% conversion)

### Stage 3: Decision (Your offer)

**Goal**: They choose to buy from you

**How**:
- Crystal clear offer
- Social proof
- Easy to understand pricing
- Simple buying process
- Clear next step

**Success metric**: 10-20% of interested prospects convert to customers

## Funnel Optimization (Do This After You Launch)

### What to Track

- **Visitor count**: How many people see your content?
- **Signup rate**: % of visitors who opt-in for lead magnet?
- **Email open rate**: % of emails opened (target: 30%)
- **Email click rate**: % who clicked (target: 5%)
- **Conversion rate**: % of leads who become customers (target: 5-10%)

### What to Test

**Lead magnet**: Test different versions. Use the one with highest signup rate.

**Email subject lines**: "Question" vs "Here's what works" - see which gets more opens.

**Page headline**: "How to get first 10 clients" vs "Done-for-you client acquisition" - which resonates?

**CTA button**: "Learn more" vs "Schedule call" - which converts better?

**Offer structure**: "Pay once" vs "Pay monthly" - what sells more?

## Common Funnel Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No Lead Magnet
"Just follow me and I'll send something"
Result: 0.5% signup rate vs 10%+ with good magnet

### Mistake 2: Broken Funnel
Lead magnet doesn't connect to your offer
Result: Leads leave confused

### Mistake 3: No Follow-up
One email then silence
Result: Leads go cold

### Mistake 4: Too Many Options
"Here are 5 service packages"
Result: Analysis paralysis, fewer sales

### Mistake 5: No Social Proof
No testimonials, reviews, case studies
Result: People don't trust you
