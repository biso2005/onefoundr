# Best Business Models for Solopreneurs: Which One Fits You

A business model is how you make money.

You could be talented, but if your model doesn't fit your lifestyle/skills, you'll struggle.

There are really only 8 business models for solopreneurs. Let's go through each.

---

## The 8 Business Models

### Model 1: Freelancing / Services

You trade TIME for MONEY.

**How it works:**
- You have a skill (writing, design, development, coaching)
- Someone pays you for that skill
- You deliver the work
- Repeat

**Examples:**
- Freelance designer ($150/hour)
- Virtual assistant ($25/hour)
- Consultant ($500-5,000/project)

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
6-18 months (start at $30/hr, scale to $150+/hr)

**Pros:**
- Start immediately (no product needed)
- Reliable income
- Proven model
- Easy to test

**Cons:**
- Time-capped (can only work so many hours)
- Doesn't scale without raising rates dramatically
- Burnout risk
- Income stops when you stop working
- Bottleneck is you

**Best for:**
People with specific skills, don't mind 1:1 client work, won't mind raising rates dramatically to scale

---

### Model 2: Productized Services

You take a service, package it, and sell it fixed-price repeatable.

**How it works:**
- You offer service XYZ
- You package it exactly: $2,000 for exact deliverables
- Every client gets the same process
- Efficiency = profit

**Examples:**
- Website design ($3,000 fixed)
- Logo design ($5,000 fixed)
- 90-min strategy sessions ($500)
- Sales page writing ($2,500)

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
6-12 months (5 clients at $2,000 each)

**Pros:**
- More scalable than pure services
- Faster delivery (since standardized)
- Can systematize and eventually delegate
- Clients like fixed price
- Higher profit margins

**Cons:**
- Still requires YOU initially
- Harder to scale beyond you
- Need to say no to custom work
- Some clients want customization

**Best for:**
People with a specific skill who want \$5k-20k/month without building a team

---

### Model 3: Coaching / Consulting

You provide expert guidance to help someone achieve a goal.

**How it works:**
- 1:1 calls with clients
- You guide them toward goal
- Usually recurring (monthly retainer or package)
- Higher price point

**Examples:**
- Business coaching ($2,000/month)
- Career coaching ($500/month)
- Health coaching ($300/month)
- Writing coaching ($1,000/month)

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
4-12 months (5 coaching clients at $2,000/month)

**Pros:**
- Highest hourly rate (\$200-500+/hr)
- Faster to $10k (fewer clients needed)
- Recurring revenue = predictable
- Relationship-based (less commoditized)

**Cons:**
- Requires deep expertise
- Emotionally demanding
- Still capped at number of hours
- Requires strong results (testimonials matter)

**Best for:**
Experts with track record who are great at helping people achieve goals and like direct relationships

---

### Model 4: Digital Products

You create something once, sell it infinite times.

**How it works:**
- Create course, template, checklist, or app
- Price it ($17-500)
- Sell it online (passive income)
- You make 100% margin

**Examples:**
- Online course ($297)
- Email template bundle ($37)
- Spreadsheet tool ($49)
- Notion template ($39)

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
12-24 months (need 2,000+ sales of $5-10 product, or 100 sales of $100 product)

**Pros:**
- Truly passive (after initial creation)
- Highest leverage
- No scaling/delivery challenges
- Can be sold forever

**Cons:**
- Takes time to build
- Requires marketing/audience
- Harder to launch without audience
- Completion is hard
- Can't charge premium without audience

**Best for:**
People patient enough to spend 6+ months building without immediate income, have/are building audience

---

### Model 5: SaaS (Software as a Service)

You build software, charge monthly subscription.

**How it works:**
- Build tool that solves problem
- Charge monthly ($10-500/month)
- Scale with customers
- Recurring revenue

**Examples:**
- CRM ($29/month)
- Email deliverability tool ($49/month)
- Scheduling software ($99/month)

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
18-36 months (need 100+ customers at $100/month)

**Pros:**
- True passive income
- Predictable recurring revenue
- Scales infinitely
- High margins (software costs nothing per customer)

**Cons:**
- Requires technical skill (or hiring developer = expensive)
- Competitive space
- Maintenance required
- Takes 18+ months to $10k
- Constant competition

**Best for:**
Technical founders who can build, willing to wait 2-3 years for real revenue

---

### Model 6: Affiliate Marketing / Sponsorships

You recommend other products. You make commission or sponsorship fee.

**How it works:**
- Build audience (newsletter, YouTube, blog, podcast)
- Recommend products relevant to them
- Make commission (5-30% typically)
- Or get paid for sponsoring

**Examples:**
- Write about tools, link to affiliate link (earn 20% commission)
- Sponsor your newsletter ($2,000/sponsor)
- YouTube affiliate commissions

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
12-24 months (need audience of 5,000-10,000+ engaged followers)

**Pros:**
- Lower barrier (no product to build)
- Passive (once audience is built)
- Can diversify income
- Lean in to what you already enjoy

**Cons:**
- Takes time to build audience first
- Requires audience trust
- Income depends on others' products
- Saturated space

**Best for:**
Builders/creators who love content and already have audience, or willing to build one

---

### Model 7: Membership / Subscription

You provide ongoing value. People pay monthly to access.

**How it works:**
- Build exclusive content
- Charge monthly subscription
- Deliver content, access, community
- Scale with members

**Examples:**
- Membership community ($49/month)
- Exclusive coaching community ($297/month)
- Premium newsletter ($15/month)

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
12-18 months (need 40-100 members depending on price)

**Pros:**
- Recurring revenue
- Community aspect (members support each other)
- Scales beyond you
- Engaged customers

**Cons:**
- Requires content/value every month (ongoing work)
- Harder to find initial members
- Churn is real (monthly cancellations)
- High support burden

**Best for:**
Content creators, community builders who enjoy ongoing interaction

---

### Model 8: Hybrid / Multiple Streams

You mix 2-3 models to diversify.

**How it works:**
- Services ($5k/month) + Digital product ($3k/month) + Affiliate ($2k/month) = $10k/month
- Less dependent on one income source
- Less likely to hit ceiling on one

**Examples:**
- Consultant + Course
- Coach + Membership
- Freelancer + Affiliate + Digital product

**Timeline to $10k/month:**
6-18 months (faster than single model)

**Pros:**
- More resilient (if one drops, others support)
- Faster to $10k (diversified)
- Can leverage one thing to grow another

**Cons:**
- More complex to manage
- Attention divided
- Can't go super deep in anything

**Best for:**
Experienced entrepreneurs who want faster growth and resilience

## Which Model Should YOU Use?

### If you want money FAST (3-6 months): 
**Services** or **Productized Services**

### If you want PASSIVE income:
**Digital Products**, **SaaS**, **Affiliate**, **Membership**

### If you want high HOURLY RATE:
**Coaching**, **Consulting**

### If you want to LEVERAGE time:
**Digital Products**, **SaaS**, **Membership**

### If you want control:
**Services**, **Digital Products**, **Membership**

### If you want PREDICTABLE income:
**Coaching**, **Membership**, **SaaS**

### If you DON'T have audience yet:
**Services** (fastest), then build audience → do affiliate/digital products

### If you HAVE audience:
**Affiliate**, **Digital Products**, **Membership**

---

## The Sequence That Works Best

For most people, the sequence is:

**Year 1: Services or Productized Services**
- Generate income immediately
- Build case studies
- Understand your audience deeply

**Year 2: Add Digital Product**
- Leverage your audience
- Create passive income stream
- Test what people want

**Year 3+: Scale the Model**
- Double down on what works
- Potentially hire help on services
- Grow digital product
- Add affiliate/sponsorship to newsletter

This allows you to bootstrap without outside funding.

---

## Your Choice

Pick ONE model to start.

Don't try hybrid right away (too complex).

Do that model for 6-12 months.

Once you have cash flow and understand business better, add another.

Most successful solopreneurs start with services, then layer in products.

Start there if unsure.
