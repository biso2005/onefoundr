## The Opportunity

Entrepreneurs struggle with admin work. A competent VA who can handle email, scheduling, research, and customer service is worth thousands per month because they free up the founder's expensive time.

## The Economics

**Hourly:** $25-50/hour
**Monthly retainer:** $2,000-4,000 for 20-30 hours/week
**Specialization boost:** VAs who specialize (e.g., e-commerce ops, SaaS onboarding, coaching admin) charge 2-3x more

## The Model

Start as a generalist VA. After 3-6 months, specialize:
- **E-commerce VA:** Manage inventory, fulfill orders, customer service for Shopify stores
- **SaaS VA:** Handle customer onboarding, support, billing
- **Coaching VA:** Schedule clients, manage email, handle invoicing
- **Agency VA:** Project coordination, client communication, team scheduling

Specialization lets you charge $40-60/hour instead of $20-30.

## Real Timeline

Month 1: Offer VA services at $25/hour
Find 2-3 clients at 10 hours/week = $500-750/week

Month 3: Raise rates to $35/hour based on results
Streamline processes, take on more clients

Month 6: Specialize in one niche, raise to $50/hour or retainer at $3,000/month

## Getting Started

1. Document what you're best at (email, scheduling, bookkeeping, research, etc.)
2. Find one ideal client type (SaaS founders, coaches, e-commerce)
3. Pitch to 15-20 prospects
4. Land first client with 5-10 hours/week trial
5. Deliver excellence: become their person

## The Key

Be PROACTIVE. Don't wait for instructions:
- Anticipate needs before they ask
- Build systems to reduce back-and-forth
- Take ownership of your domain

This turns contractors into indispensable employees (without the equity).

## Scale Plan

- Move to retainer pricing (more predictable)
- Specialize to $4,000-5,000/month per client
- Add a junior VA and manage their work
- Expand service offerings (bookkeeping, HR basics, tech setup)
