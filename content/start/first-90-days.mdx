# The Solo Founder's First 90 Days: Your Roadmap to Launch

The first 90 days of a solo business are critical.

Not because you need to make money (though that's nice).

Because you need to learn: Do people actually want this?

Most founders waste the first 90 days building perfection. By day 91, they've built something no one wants.

Smart founders spend 90 days validating: Do my customers actually need this?

---

## The 90-Day Goal

By day 90, you should have:

✅ Validated the problem exists (via customer interviews)
✅ Validated people want your solution (via conversations)
✅ Built minimum version of your offering
✅ Sold to 1-3 first customers
✅ Proven you can deliver
✅ Learned what matters to customers

Money is secondary. Learning is primary.

---

## Days 1-15: Foundation

### Day 1-3: Define Your Idea

**What to do:**
- Write down: What problem do I solve?
- Who has this problem?
- How do I solve it better than alternatives?
- Why am I credible?

**Deliverable:** 1-page description of your business

### Day 4-7: Research Your Market

**What to do:**
- Google your niche
- Find 10 people in your target market
- Look at what competitors charge
- Find 3 Facebook groups where your audience hangs out
- Subscribe to 2-3 newsletters in your space

**Deliverable:** Market research document (5 pages max)

### Day 8-10: Create Simple Positioning

**What to do:**
- Write your elevator pitch: "I help [audience] [achieve outcome] by [method]"
- Test it on 3 people. Do they understand?
- Refine based on feedback

**Example:**
"I help freelance designers transition to value-based pricing so they can work less and earn more."

**Deliverable:** Positioning statement (1-2 paragraphs)

### Day 11-15: Set Up Minimal Website

**What to do:**
- Create simple landing page (Carrd, Webflow, Wix, even notion)
- Include: Headline, who it's for, what you do, how to contact you
- Nothing fancy. Functional > beautiful
- Get custom domain

**Deliverable:** Functional website with contact form

## Days 16-45: Validation

This is THE most important phase. Talk to potential customers.

### Day 16-30: Customer Interviews (10 Interviews)

**What to do:**
- Reach out to 20 people who fit your target market
- Offer 30-min call (no pitch, no selling)
- Ask them about their problem
- Listen way more than talk
- Record key insights

**Interview Questions (suggested):**
1. Tell me about your biggest challenge with [topic]
2. How much time/money does this cost you?
3. What have you tried to solve it?
4. How bad is it right now? (1-10 scale)
5. Would you pay someone to solve this?

**Deliverable:** Interview notes from 10 conversations

**Red Flags:** 
- Same person doesn't mention your problem = demand may not exist
- People say "I'd buy" but won't take your call = not urgent
- People actively searching for solution = perfect sign

### Day 31-45: Build Lean Version

Based on interviews, build simplest possible version.

**If you're offering a service:**
- Write down your exact 3-step process
- Create simple one-pager describing it
- Create PDF template you'll use

**If you're offering a course:**
- Outline the exact curriculum
- Record video lessons (phone camera is fine)
- Create simple workbook

**If you're offering software:**
- Design 3 core screens wireframes
- Use no-code tool (Bubble, Webflow) to quick build
- Make it functional, not beautiful

**Rule:** If it takes more than 4 weeks to build, you're building too much.

**Deliverable:** MVP (Minimum Viable Product) - simplest version you can offer

## Days 46-60: First Sales

### Day 46-50: Create Sales Page

**What to do:**
- Write sales page or email sequence
- Headline: Problem they have
- How you solve it
- Social proof (testimonials, case studies if you have them)
- Pricing (clear, not ambiguous)
- Call to action (clear next step)

Learn from your interviews. Use language they used.

### Day 51-60: Sell to First 3 Customers

**How:**
- Email your interview contacts: "Based on our chat about [problem], I created [solution]. Interested? $[price]"
- Post in relevant communities about what you built
- Tell your network
- Direct outreach via LinkedIn/email

**Goal:** 1-3 customers committed

**Pricing:**
- First customers get discount ($500-2,000 is good starting point)
- You want to learn from them

**Objections you'll hear:**
- "Let me think about it..." → Set follow-up date
- "It's too expensive" → Ask: What's your budget?
- "I need to try it free first" → "Let's schedule a call to see if this is a fit"

**Accept by day 60:** 1+ customer paid

**Reframe:** Even 1 customer paying = validation that your idea works

---

## Days 61-75: Deliver & Learn

### Day 61-70: Onboard First Customers

**What to do:**
- Get them started on your service/product
- Check in daily (not pushy, just present)
- Ask for feedback constantly
- Take notes on what's working/broken

**Questions to ask:**
- What's working well so far?
- What's confusing?
- What would make this 10x better?
- Would you recommend this to others?

### Day 71-75: Iterate Based on Feedback

**What to do:**
- Fix what's broken
- Double down on what works
- Update your offering based on their feedback
- Prepare for customer 4-5

---

## Days 76-90: Scale & Refine

### Day 76-85: Get Testimonials

**What to do:**
- Ask first customers for testimonial
- Ask: What's the #1 thing this solved for you?
- Get permission to use name/title/photo
- Add to website and sales materials

**Good testimonial:**
"[Name], [Title/Company] - '[Specific result]. This saved me [time/money]. I recommend it to anyone who [situation].'"

### Day 86-90: Plan for Year 2

**What to do:**
- Review: What worked? What didn't?
- Adjust positioning
- Plan: How do I get 5 more customers next month?
- Think: Do I keep this niche or pivot?

**Questions:**
- Would I want 10 customers like these?
- Can I do this sustainably?
- Is the money sufficient?
- Am I actually solving the right problem?

## Common Mistakes in First 90 Days

### Mistake 1: Over-Building

You spend 60 days building perfect product. On day 61, realize no one wants it.

Instead: Validate first, build second.

### Mistake 2: Not Talking to Customers

You assume you know what they want. You don't.

Instead: Talk to 10 people. Then build.

### Mistake 3: Wrong Initial Price

You price too cheap ("I'm new") or too high ("My value is high").

Instead: Price at value. Get feedback. Adjust.

### Mistake 4: No Positioning

You're "helping people succeed." Everyone is.

Instead: Narrow down who/what. Be specific.

### Mistake 5: Giving Up Too Early

"No one bought in week 2. This doesn't work."

Instead: First customer takes 4-8 weeks. That's normal.

## Your 90-Day Goal

Day 1: Excited, nervous, no customers
Day 90: 1-3 customers, learned a ton, validated idea works

This is success. Real money comes later.

The first 90 days are about learning and validation.

Execute this roadmap. You'll know on day 90 if this is real or not.

And you'll have a foundation to build the next year on.
