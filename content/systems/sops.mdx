A Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is a documented step-by-step guide for how you do something in your business.

Most solopreneurs don't have SOPs. Everything lives in their head. When they take time off, nothing gets done. If they want to hire help, it's chaos because they have to explain everything from scratch. But the real problem is that without SOPs, you can't implement [automation tools](/tools/ai-tools) that replace manual work — you first need to document the work.

SOPs fix this. They're your business's memory.

## Why Solopreneurs Need SOPs

You probably think: "I'm just one person. Why do I need documentation?"

Here's why:

1. **Memory is unreliable**: You won't remember exactly how you onboard clients in 6 months. Documentation keeps it consistent.

2. **Delegation**: The moment you want to hire help or outsource work, you need SOPs. Otherwise, you spend weeks teaching.

3. **Quality control**: When processes are documented, you notice gaps and inefficiencies.

4. **Time savings**: Documented workflows often reveal shortcuts. Once documented, you can optimize.

5. **Scaling velocity**: With SOPs, you can add contractors strategically instead of hiring full-time.

6. **Peace of mind**: You know your business runs smoothly even if you're away for a week.

## What to Document First

Start with your most important and repetitive processes:

- **Client onboarding**: How you get a new client set up (covered in your [productized service](/offer/productized-services) scope)
- **Delivery process**: How you do your core work
- **Payment/invoicing**: How you bill and collect payment
- **Quality checks**: How you ensure quality before delivering
- **Follow-up**: How you stay in touch with past clients

Don't document everything. Start with the top 3-5 most important workflows.

## SOP Template Structure

Use this structure for every SOP:

### [Name of Process]

**Purpose**: One sentence. Why do we do this?
Example: "To ensure every client has a smooth first experience and knows what to expect."

**Owner**: Who is responsible for this? (You, or name of person if delegated)

**Frequency**: How often does this happen? (Daily, weekly, per client, etc.)

**Time estimate**: How long does this take? (30 minutes, 2 hours, etc.)

**Tools needed**: What software/tools are required?
Example: Stripe, Gmail, Airtable, Google Docs

**Steps**:

1. [First action]
   - Details/notes
   - Any conditional logic (if X happens, do Y)

2. [Second action]

3. [Continue for all steps]

**Checklist**: A clean checklist version someone can execute from

**Common issues**: What usually goes wrong?

**Quality checkpoint**: How do you know this was done right?

## Example SOP

### **Client Onboarding**

**Purpose**: To set up a new client for success and establish clear expectations for the engagement.

**Owner**: [Your name]

**Frequency**: Per new client

**Time estimate**: 30 minutes

**Tools needed**: Gmail, Airtable, Google Docs, Stripe

**Steps**:

1. **Send welcome email** (template in Airtable)
   - Include: project timeline, deliverables, what they need to provide, how to reach you
   - Attach: contract template, invoice, any intake forms

2. **Schedule kickoff call**
   - Offer 3 time slots
   - 30-min call on Zoom
   - Send calendar invite with Zoom link in email

3. **Prepare before kickoff call**
   - Review their intake form
   - Note any questions or red flags
   - Pull up their project details

4. **Run kickoff call** (follow agenda below)
   - Intro: who you are, what you do (2 min)
   - Their goals: "What does success look like?" (5 min)
   - Your process: walk them through timeline (5 min)
   - Next steps: when you'll send first draft, when you expect feedback (3 min)
   - Q&A (10 min)

5. **Send kickoff summary email**
   - Recap what you discussed
   - Next steps and dates
   - Their deliverables/deadlines
   - Invoice (payment due before work starts)

6. **Create project folder** in Google Drive with their name
   - Share with client
   - Include: shared document, timeline, resources

7. **Mark in Airtable** as "Active" and set delivery date reminder

**Checklist**:
- [ ] Welcome email sent
- [ ] Kickoff call scheduled
- [ ] Intake form reviewed
- [ ] Kickoff call completed
- [ ] Summary email sent
- [ ] Project folder created and shared
- [ ] Invoice sent
- [ ] Airtable updated

**Common issues**:
- Client doesn't fill out intake form before call (Send reminder 24 hours before)
- Scope creep in initial call (Stick to script, say "Let's discuss that once we start")
- Client forgets Zoom link (Send reminder 1 hour before)

**Quality checkpoint**:
- Client has signed contract ✓
- Client has paid invoice ✓
- Project is in Google Drive ✓
- Client knows timeline ✓

## How to Create SOPs

1. **Do the process yourself** while taking notes
   - Write down every single step, even the obvious ones
   - Note where decisions happen ("If client says X, do Y")
   - Time how long it takes

2. **Write rough draft** in Google Docs
   - Don't make it pretty yet. Just get it down.

3. **Do it again** while following your draft
   - See what's missing
   - See what's unclear
   - Refine

4. **Have someone else do it** using your SOP
   - Best test: can someone who doesn't know you execute it correctly?
   - Note what's confusing
   - Refine again

5. **Keep a master SOP document**
   - Google Drive folder with all SOPs
   - Update whenever you change a process
   - Version control (V1, V2, etc.) for major changes

## Tools for SOPs

You don't need fancy software.

- **Google Docs**: Free, easy to collaborate
- **Notion**: Better for linking related processes
- **Airtable**: If you want to manage processes as data
- **Loom**: Screen recordings of how to do something visually
- **Asana/Monday**: If you want to turn SOPs into task lists

Most solopreneurs do fine with Google Docs.

- Proposal process

- Customer support
- Inventory check

## The Hidden Benefit

Most solopreneurs discover something unexpected: documenting your process reveals **inefficiencies you never saw**.

You might realize:
- You send 5 emails when 2 would do
- You check something twice
- You're using 2 tools when 1 would work
- A step takes 30 minutes but could take 10 with a template

SOPs are forcing functions for continuous improvement.

## Start Today

Pick your most important process. Spend 2 hours documenting it. Then try delegating it to someone.

If they can do it without asking you questions, your SOP is good.

If they ask questions, your SOP needs clarification.

This feedback loop — document → test → refine — is how you build a business that doesn't depend entirely on you.
