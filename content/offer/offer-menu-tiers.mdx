## The One-Offer Problem

You have one offer. One price. One option.

Your price-conscious prospects think: "I want to work with you, but I can't afford it." So they leave.

Your ambitious prospects think: "This is good, but I need more depth." So they negotiate or ghoste.

And you're leaving money on the table because some customers would pay more for deeper service—you just never asked.

This is the one-offer ceiling. Most solopreneurs hit it around $50-100K annual revenue, then get stuck.

The solution isn't to drop your price or create ten different packages. It's to create three tiers that serve different segments without diluting your positioning.

---

## Why Three Tiers Work Better Than One

Here's the math:

**With one offer:**
- 30% of potential customers are priced out (want less, can't afford full price)
- 30% want more depth but accept your baseline
- 40% are your ideal fit

**With three tiers:**
- 30% buy Tier 1 (the starter) at 1/3 your price
- 50% buy Tier 2 (your current offer) at full price
- 20% buy Tier 3 (premium) at 2-3x your price

Revenue math:
- One offer: 40% × 1.0 = 0.40 (your baseline)
- Three tiers: (30% × 0.33) + (50% × 1.0) + (20% × 2.5) = 0.10 + 0.50 + 0.50 = **1.1**

Just from segmentation, you've increased your average revenue by 2-3x. Not because you're working more. Because you're capturing more value from each segment.

But the real advantage is psychological:

When someone sees three options, your middle tier suddenly looks like the smart choice. You've created an anchor (the premium tier) that makes your standard tier feel like good value.

This is anchoring bias, and it works. Restaurants do it with wine lists. SaaS does it with pricing tiers. You should too.

## The 3-Tier Framework

### Tier 1: The Starter (30% of customers, 20% of revenue)

**What it is:** Entry point. Self-guided with light support.

**What's included:**
- Your core framework (the knowledge)
- Minimal 1-on-1 support (maybe one call or email support)
- DIY execution (they do most of the work)

**Price:** 30-40% of your full offer
- If your standard is $5,000, Tier 1 is $1,500-2,000

**Who buys this:**
- Budget-conscious prospects (can't afford full service)
- Self-directed learners (want to do it themselves)
- People testing whether your approach works before investing more

**Example:**
- Coaching: DIY course ($1,500) vs. standard coaching
- Copywriting: Swipe file + templates ($2,000) vs. full copy project
- Design: Template kit + critique ($1,500) vs. custom design
- Marketing consulting: Framework guide ($2,500) vs. full strategy + implementation

**Why this works:** You convert people who were going to be "no" anyway. They weren't your ideal clients (not engaged enough to pay full price), but they're still customers. And many convert to Tier 2 later.

---

### Tier 2: The Standard (50% of customers, 50% of revenue)

**What it is:** Your current offer. The sweet spot.

**What's included:**
- Everything Tier 1 has, plus:
- Meaningful 1-on-1 support (multiple calls, structured feedback)
- Implementation support (you guide them through execution)
- Customization (adapted to their specific situation)

**Price:** 1.0x (your current rate)
- If you charge $5,000, keep it at $5,000

**Who buys this:**
- Your ideal clients (they match your positioning)
- People who want depth but aren't in "white-glove" territory
- Clients who want guidance but will do the work

**Why this works:** This is your bread and butter. Most customers fit here. By keeping this as your "standard," you're not discounting from your real value—you're tiering intelligently.

---

### Tier 3: The Premium (20% of customers, 30% of revenue)

**What it is:** White-glove, concierge, deep service.

**What's included:**
- Everything Tier 2 has, plus:
- Priority access (you're available when they need you)
- Done-for-you elements (you do some of the work, not just guide)
- Deep customization (tailored extensively to their situation)
- Faster turnaround (expedited delivery)
- Extra touchpoints (more frequent check-ins)

**Price:** 150-250% of Tier 2
- If Tier 2 is $5,000, Tier 3 is $7,500-12,500

**Who buys this:**
- Players with budget (they value speed and execution)
- Urgent situations (people who need results ASAP)
- Risk-averse clients (they want you involved heavily)
- Repeat customers (they know you deliver, so they invest more)

**Why this works:** This tier funds your business lifestyle. One Tier 3 client is worth 2-3 Tier 2 clients in revenue, but often needs less delivery time (because they give you more freedom to execute). Also, Tier 3 clients are more satisfied (you're deeply involved) and more likely to refer.

## How to Build Your Tiers in Practice

**Step 1: Start with Tier 2 (your current offer)**
- You already know this works
- You know your pricing
- You know the delivery

Don't change it yet.

**Step 2: Create Tier 1 by removing 40% of what you do**

Ask: What's the 60% that produces 80% of results?

- Coaching: What's the core framework? That's your DIY tier. The support/accountability is the premium.
- Copywriting: What's the core technique? That's your swipe file tier. Custom research/strategy is the premium.
- Design: What's the core concept? That's your template tier. Custom design/build is the premium.

Remove the support, customization, and implementation hand-holding. Keep the knowledge.

**Step 3: Create Tier 3 by adding support and speed**

Ask: What could you add that would justify 2-3x pricing?

- More availability (priority support, faster response)
- More done-for-you (you do implementation, not just advise)
- More customization (deeper interview, more iterations)
- More frequent touchpoints (weekly check-ins instead of monthly)

**Step 4: Test your tiers**

Launch all three and see which attracts what. You'll get:
- Data on actual customer preferences
- Clarity on which tier actually pays best
- Feedback on pricing (are people balking at Tier 1? At Tier 3?)

Be willing to adjust. Maybe Tier 3 should be 2x instead of 2.5x. Maybe Tier 1 should be 40% instead of 33%. Data beats guessing.

## Specific Examples That Show It Works

**Example 1: Copywriting Coach**

Current: One package. $3,000 for 1 project + 3 revision rounds. Client does implementation.

**Tier 1 ($999):** 
- DIY email framework (mini-course)
- Critique of one email they've written (paid critique service)
- No revisions, no ongoing support
- They execute everything themselves

**Tier 2 ($3,000):** 
- Current package (unchanged)
- Copywriting + 3 revision rounds
- Implementation is their job, but you guide

**Tier 3 ($7,500):** 
- Copy + revisions (unlimited rounds)
- You help implement (not just hand it off)
- Monthly refinement calls (optimize based on performance)
- Higher priority (you respond within 24 hours)
- Results-based pricing (you see what hit, you refine for next month)

Revenue impact: If she did 10 clients/year at $3,000, that's $30K. With tiering, it's likely (4 × $999) + (5 × $3,000) + (1 × $7,500) = $28.5K... wait, that dipped.

But here's what actually happens: You get MORE clients because Tier 1 is attractive. You also get clients willing to pay Tier 3 because it's a clear premium option. The real math is (8 × $999) + (10 × $3,000) + (3 × $7,500) = $56,487. Almost 2x revenue.

**Example 2: Business Operations Consultant**

Current: One package. $5,000 for 6-week optimization project. You do the audit and recommend systems; they implement.

**Tier 1 ($1,500):**
- 4-week DIY systems audit (framework guide)
- They do the auditing, you give them the questions
- Final feedback on their plan (review only)
- No implementation support

**Tier 2 ($5,000):**
- Current package (unchanged)
- You do the audit, they implement

**Tier 3 ($12,000):**
- Full implementation (you set up systems, not just recommend them)
- Ongoing check-ins (3 months of optimization, not just initial setup)
- Integration with their team (training on new systems)
- Done-for-you calendar audit, process documentation, everything

Revenue example: 8 clients @ $5K = $40K. With tiering: (6 @ $1,500) + (8 @ $5,000) + (2 @ $12,000) = $59K. 50% revenue increase.

## Why Tiering Isn't Nickel-and-Diming

Some solopreneurs worry: "If I create a cheaper tier, will clients think I'm watering down my offer?"

No. Here's why:

Tier 1 isn't a diluted version of your offer. It's a *different service for a different segment*. Your premium clients know the difference between "DIY framework with light support" and "full white-glove service." They're not offended by the budget option; they're reassured that there IS a premium option.

In fact, tiering makes your premium tier more valuable:
- It shows your work is worth graduating into
- It creates aspirational positioning (if they can't afford Tier 3 now, maybe later)
- It anchors your pricing (Tier 3 makes Tier 2 look reasonable)

The clients who buy Tier 1 are mostly people who wouldn't have bought anything. They're new revenue, not discounted revenue.

## The Financial Reality

When you implement tiering correctly, here's what usually happens:

**Tier 1 attracts:** Curious prospects, budget-conscious segments, self-directed people
- Conversion rate: Usually 40-60% of inquiries (because price is low, commitment is light)
- Upside: High volume, many eventually convert to Tier 2

**Tier 2 (your standard) remains:** Your ideal clients
- Conversion rate: Usually 30-40% (unchanged from before)
- Upside: You're not discounting your bread-and-butter

**Tier 3 attracts:** Urgent situations, repeat clients, budget-available segment
- Conversion rate: Usually 5-10% (lower volume, but much higher value)
- Upside: These are your best clients (more satisfied, more referrals, more lifetime value)

Combined effect: You're pulling in volume at the bottom, stability in the middle, and margin at the top.

This is how established service businesses scale without burning out.

## Your Tiering Checklist

**Before you launch your tiers:**

- [ ] Tier 1 is simple enough that people feel confident doing it with light support
- [ ] Tier 2 is unchanged from what works for you now (keep the cash cow)
- [ ] Tier 3 adds meaningful support/speed/customization (not just more hours at your standard rate)
- [ ] Prices reflect actual value difference (Tier 1 is materially different service at lower price)
- [ ] You can deliver Tier 3 profitably at the price point
- [ ] Your messaging differentiates clearly between tiers
- [ ] You've thought about which tier is your ideal, and you're okay if most clients are still there

**When you launch:**

- [ ] Start with Tier 2 messaging (keep your positioning)
- [ ] Quietly introduce Tier 1 (it's there if prospects ask or balk at price)
- [ ] Lead with Tier 3 (position premium first to anchor Tier 2 as "reasonable")
- [ ] Track which tier attracts which segment (you'll learn a lot)

## The Bigger Picture

Tiering isn't about making more money (though you will).

It's about serving more people, the ones who can't afford your full package today but want to work with you anyway.

It's about creating stability in revenue (Tier 2) while building premium relationships (Tier 3) that compound.

It's about moving from "one offer, one price, one client type" to "intelligent market segmentation that makes sense for your business."

Three tiers. One philosophy. More revenue.

That's how you get from stuck at $50K to thriving at $100K+.
