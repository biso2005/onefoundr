## Why Services Don't Scale (But Products Do)

You trade time for money. Every hour you work, you earn. Every hour you don't work, you earn nothing.

That's the services ceiling.

But here's the secret: the most successful solopreneurs don't go straight from hourly services to digital products. They first create a [productized service](/offer/productized-services) that maintains high margins while reducing the service delivery burden. Then, once they understand their customer deeply, they [convert that expertise into digital products](/offer/service-to-product) that scale infinitely.

But most solopreneurs never build a digital product because they think it requires:
- Technical skills (you think you need to code)
- Huge audiences (you think you need 10,000 followers)
- Complex platforms (you think you need to be a marketer)

None of that is true.

A digital product is just **packaged expertise delivered without your presence**.

## The Digital Product Types (Pick One)

### 1. Courses (The Popular Choice)

**What it is:** Recorded lessons + workbook + community/support

**Why solopreneurs choose it:** Feels like a "real" product. High perceived value.

**Reality:**
- Takes 2-4 months to make
- Hard to keep updated
- High completion rates are rare (5-10% typical)
- Works if you have an audience

**Price range:** $97-$997

**Best for:** You have people who already know/trust you. If you don't have an audience yet, focus on [getting your first paying clients through discovery calls](/sales/discovery-calls) so you understand your market deeply before creating a course.

**Example:** Copywriting course ($297) for newsletter writers

### 2. Templates (The Quick Win)

**What it is:** Pre-built systems people can customize (Notion templates, Figma designs, spreadsheets)

**Why solopreneurs choose it:** Fastest to create (1-2 weeks). Evergreen. No updates needed.

**Reality:**
- Low price = volume game
- Saturated market for some templates
- Works with zero audience (marketplace discovery)
- Can be created while still doing client work

**Price range:** $7-$49

**Best for:** Systems-oriented people. You've already built something useful.

**Example:** "Notion CRM for freelancers" template ($29) sold on Gumroad

### 3. Ebooks (The Underrated Option)

**What it is:** Long-form guide sold as PDF/Kindle book

**Why choose it:** Fast to create (3-6 weeks). No production overhead. Credibility.

**Reality:**
- Medium price point
- Lower perceived value than courses
- But WAY less work than courses
- Works great when you have an email list
- Pairs perfectly with courses (upsell)

**Price range:** $27-$97

**Best for:** Writers. People with email lists.

**Example:** "The Pricing Ebook" ($47) - comprehensive guide to pricing your offer

### 4. Software/Tools (The Technical Path)

**What it is:** SaaS, Chrome extension, plugin, app

**Why choose it:** High perceived value. Recurring revenue potential. Hard to compete with.

**Reality:**
- Hardest to build (unless you code or hire)
- Easiest to sell (high perceived value)
- Takes 2-6 months minimum
- Technical support burden
- BUT: Monthly recurring = predictable income

**Price range:** $9-$99/month (recurring)

**Best for:** You can code OR you can hire a developer affordably

**Example:** SEO audit tool ($29/month) for agencies

### 5. Communities (The Hybrid)

**What it is:** Membership community (Slack/Circle/Discord) + resources

**Why choose it:** Recurring revenue. Retention is high (people feel invested). Network effects.

**Reality:**
- Requires consistent moderation/engagement
- Hidden labor cost (you manage the community)
- Works with 50-200 members (small and tight)
- Don't start here (start with templates, then upgrade)

**Price range:** $9-$99/month (recurring)

**Best for:** You enjoy community building. You want relationship + product hybrid.

**Example:** Freelancer accountability group ($49/month) with weekly calls + resources

## The $0-to-$1000 Digital Product Playbook

### Week 1: Choose Your Asset

**Pick one:**
1. What have you already built for clients? (Template it)
2. What problem do you solve repeatedly? (Course it)
3. What knowledge could you document? (Ebook it)
4. What tool would you build if you had infinite time? (Software it)

Choose based on:
- **Fastest:** Template (1-2 weeks)
- **Safest:** Ebook (3-6 weeks)
- **Most Profitable:** Software (but takes longest)
- **Most Work:** Course (2-4 months)

**Action:** Decide TODAY. Pick your format.

### Week 2-3: Create or Package

**For Templates:**
- Document the system you use
- Record a quick walkthrough (5-10 min)
- Package as Figma file, spreadsheet, Notion page
- Create a 1-page guide

**For Ebooks:**
- Outline the biggest problem you solve
- Write 20-40 pages (~5,000-10,000 words)
- Design a simple cover (Canva)
- Export to PDF

**For Courses:**
- Film 8-12 lesson videos (10-15 min each)
- Create a workbook (1-2 pages per lesson)
- Set up platform (teachable, Kajabi, or Gumroad)
- Record intro module fully

**For Software:**
- Build MVP (minimum viable product)
- Get 5 early users to test
- Fix critical bugs
- Document how to use it

### Week 4: Launch & Sell

**Before launch:**
- Set up sales page (Gumroad, ConvertKit, Teachable, or custom)
- Write 5-7 testimonials (ask your best clients)
- Create simple email sequence (3 emails)
- Pick launch date (this week)

**Launch day:**
- Email your list (if you have one)
- Post to your Twitter/LinkedIn
- Tell 5 people directly
- Do 3 coffee calls (introduce the product in conversation)
- Answer every question that comes in

**First 30 days:**
- Track what sells (what's the #1 question?)
- Update your sales page based on feedback
- Email your list 2-3 times (offer still available, stories from users, update)
- Iterate: make it easier, faster, or clearer

### The Next 90 Days

**Month 1-2: Optimize for Sales**
- Answer every customer question
- Collect feedback
- Update based on what customers ask
- Keep selling manually (emails, one-on-one)

**Month 2-3: Build Secondary Products**
- Start building next product (ebook leads to course, template leads to membership)
- Use feedback from first product to guide second
- Don't over-build second product (apply 80/20)

**Month 3+: Automate**
- Set up email sequences to sell automatically
- Create retargeting ads (if budget allows)
- Package second/third product
- Goal: Recurring revenue from products

## Common Digital Product Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

### Mistake 1: Building for Everyone

**You do:** Create course on "how to start a business" (50 million people)

**Reality:** 0 sales (everyone is competing, no differentiation)

**Instead:** Create on "how a copywriter can build a digital product" (specific, small, but ready to buy)

**Fix:** Narrow your audience to people with a specific problem YOU solve.

### Mistake 2: Over-Producing

**You do:** Spend 3 months filming a perfect 20-module course

**Reality:** By launch, you're exhausted, market has changed, and you've lost momentum

**Instead:** Launch 5-module course in 4 weeks, gather feedback, iterate

**Fix:** Shipping > perfect. Get feedback early. Improve often.

### Mistake 3: No Marketing Plan

**You build:** Awesome course

**You launch:** Crickets (nobody knows it exists)

**You think:** "Digital products don't work"

**Reality:** You didn't tell anyone

**Fix:** Pre-sell before you build. Email list + social presence first, product second.

### Mistake 4: Wrong Price

**Too cheap:** $7 ebook (signals low value, attracts bargain hunters)

**Too expensive:** $997 course (nobody buys without knowing you)

**Goldilocks zone:** $27-$97 for ebook, $97-$297 for course, $49 for template

**Fix:** Price 3 similar products, pick the middle, test it

### Mistake 5: Building Alone

**You think:** "I'll do all the research, creation, and marketing myself"

**Reality:** Burnout at week 4. Product never launches.

**Instead:** Outsource 1 thing (design, editing, video production)

**Fix:** For $500-1000, hire someone to do ONE task. You do the rest.

## The Math: When Digital Products Win

**Services Model (Time for Money)**
- Hourly rate: $100/hour
- Work 20 billable hours/week
- Monthly income: $8,000
- Maximum: Your time × rate

**Digital Products Model (Scale Over Time)**
- Month 1-2: Create product (no income)
- Month 3: Sell 10 copies at $97 = $970
- Month 4: Sell 20 copies at $97 = $1,940
- Month 5: Sell 30 copies at $97 = $2,910
- Month 6: Sell 50 copies at $97 = $4,850 (plus service work)
- Month 12: Sell 100 copies/month = $9,700/month (no additional time)

**Tipping point:** Month 6-9 (digital product revenue exceeds service ceiling without extra work)

## What's Next

Most solopreneurs build 1 digital product and stop.

Smart ones build 3.

- **Product 1 (Month 1-2):** Template or Ebook ($297-$2,970 revenue)
- **Product 2 (Month 3-4):** Ebook or Course ($500-$10,000 revenue)
- **Product 3 (Month 5-6):** Course or Community ($1,000-$30,000+ revenue)

Each builds on the previous. Template customers → ebook customers → course customers.

That's how you go from "$100/hour" to "$10,000/month passive."
