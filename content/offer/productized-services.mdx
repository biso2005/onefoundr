The problem with freelancing is simple: you're fixing your income to hours.

I charged $150/hour. That meant:
- 40-hour week = $6K/month
- 50-hour week = $7.5K/month (but I'd die)
- Anything more = burnout

The ceiling isn't ambition. It's physics.

Then I productized one service. Instead of "copywriting at $150/hour," I offered "Landing Page Sprint: high-converting landing page designed, written, tested in 5 days for $2,500 flat fee."

Same work. Different model.

First month: 2 landing page sprints = $5K (instead of the 33-hour equivalent = $4,950)
Second month: 4 landing page sprints = $10K 
Third month: 6 landing page sprints = $15K
Fourth month: 5 sprints + waiting list = $12.5K + $3K retainer

By month 6, I had a $15-20K/month service without working more than 40 hours/week.

This is exactly how it worked.

## The Productized Service Playbook (Tested on 4 Different Services)

### Why It Works (The Economics)

**Hourly model** (my old reality):
- Rate: $150/hour
- Billable/week: 35 hours (realistic accounting)
- Monthly revenue: $21K gross, but...
- Time to scope: 2 hours
- Time to deliver: 35 hours
- Time to admin/revisions: 3 hours
- Effective hourly rate: $150/40 total hours = $82.50/hour real

**Productized model** (landing page sprint):
- Package: $2,500 flat for landing page sprint
- Time to deliver: 20 hours (8 hours per day × 5 days)
- Time to scope: 30 min (it's predetermined)
- Time to admin: 1 hour
- Effective hourly rate: $2,500/21.5 hours = $116/hour
- But you can do 2/month same hours = $232/hour effective

**The real advantage**: Boundaries. Productized services have built-in scope, which means:
- No scope creep (price doesn't change; you don't do extra)
- Faster delivery (you know the path)
- Predictable timeline (client understands 5-day deadline)
- Easier to delegate (documented process)
- Premium positioning (fixed price = premium positioning)

## Building Yours (The 5-Step Process)

### STEP 1: Choose What to Productize (The Right Service)

Not all hourly work makes sense as productized.

**Good candidates** (I'd productize these):
- Landing page copywriting (repeatable, fixed scope, clear deliverable)
- Website design overhaul (5-10 pages, fixed timeline)
- Email sequence (defined at start: 5-email, 10-email, etc.)
- Video editing (source footage provided, editing is mechanical)
- Social media audit (fixed report structure, deliverable)
- Positioning workshop (1 day, defined outcome, 3-person team)
- Course module (defined scope: outline, script, video, workbook)

**Bad candidates** (stay hourly or do monthly retainers):
- Ongoing consulting (needs flexibility, scope evolves)
- Brand identity (takes iteration, undefined direction)
- Long-term management (changes monthly)

**How to choose**: Look at your past 20 projects. Which 3-5 had:
- Similar scope
- Clients were similarly-sized
- You knew the timeline from day 1
- Clients kept thanking you for the clarity

Pick that one.

### STEP 2: Define Your Exact Scope (No Ambiguity)

Write it out in excruciating detail. This is your protection.

**My Landing Page Sprint scope:**

**What's included:**
- 1 initial strategy call (30 min, I ask 8 specific questions)
- Audience research (I analyze 5 competitor sites + 10 customer interviews)
- Layout design: 3 sections (Hero + Problem/Solution + CTA footer)
- Copywriting: all copy (headline, subheading, body, buttons, CTA)
- Social proof section (testimonials or case studies)
- 2 rounds of revisions (not unlimited)
- Final deliverable: HTML/CSS file + editable Figma file
- 24-hour launch support (I'm available day-of launch in case issues)

**What's NOT included:**
- Filming video (you provide or hire separately)
- Graphic design beyond typography (keep it minimal = fast)
- Custom integrations (forms go to standard providers like Zapier)
- More than 2 revisions (3rd revision = $500/each)
- Hosting setup (you provide domain + hosting)

**Timeline**: 5 business days, Monday kickoff → Friday launch

**Price**: $2,500 (fixed, non-negotiable)

**Why this works**: Client knows exactly what they're getting. I know exactly what I'm delivering. No surprises.

---

### STEP 3: Price Using Value, Not Hours

**Bad pricing formula** (what most do):
- "I charge $150/hour. This takes 20 hours. So $3,000."
- Problem: Price signals low-value ("it's just my time")

**Good pricing formula** (what works):
- What's the value to the client?
- Landing page produces $100K in revenue over a year
- I typically capture 5% of that value: $5K

But I charged $2,500 because:
- It's a "sprint" (small, manageable)
- Market positioning (not premium consultancy, not cheap either)
- Profitability check: 20 hours × $125/hour effective = $2,500 ✓

### STEP 4: Document Your Delivery System (This Is Critical)

Once you've done 3, build a template.

**My landing page sprint template includes:**

**Pre-work:**
- Email template asking 8 specific questions
- Competitive analysis spreadsheet (copy their approach)
- Audience research brief (who buys? what problems?)

**Days 1-2:**
- Kickoff call (30 min)
- Layout research (sketches of 10 different layouts)
- Clients picks layout (saves 20 hours of back-and-forth)

**Days 3-4:**
- Copy all sections (headline, problem, solution, proof, CTA)
- Design in Figma (I use template from previous sprint)
- Export to HTML/CSS

**Day 5:**
- Revisions (typically 2-3 small changes)
- Final export
- Launch day support

**Admin:**
- Invoicing workflow (auto-invoice first day, 50% due, 50% on completion)
- Communication template (same emails to every client, saves time)
- Success metrics (I send email week 1: "Here's how many people visited")

**Why this works**: You're not recreating the wheel each time. You're tweaking a template. This is where your speed increases. 

First sprint: 25 hours (learning)
Second sprint: 22 hours (familiar)
Third sprint: 18 hours (now you're fast)

### STEP 5: Price Increase & Iteration Plan

Start cheaper than you think. Increase quarterly.

**My pricing trajectory:**
- Launch: $2,000 (attract first clients)
- Month 2: $2,250 (tested demand, raising)
- Month 4: $2,500 (full speed achieved, raising again)
- Month 7: $3,000 (wait list of 3 people, raise price)
- Month 10: $3,500 (could do 6/month, limiting to 4, raise price)

By month 12: 3-4 sprints/month × $3,500 = $10.5K-$14K/month revenue from 1 productized service.

Add a second service by month 8-9 (different offering, reuse brand).

- Same hourly rate baseline

## Objections & How to Handle Them

**"What if clients want customization?"**
- Your response: "Custom work is why we productize. You get our proven system. If you need full custom, that's a different engagement (longer, pricier, referred to network)."
- Reality: 95% of clients don't want "custom." They want clarity.

**"What if I'm bad at pricing?"**
- Start too low, not too high. You can always raise.
- First 3-5 clients, track actual hours religiously.
- By client 5, you'll know the real time investment.
- Then adjust pricing up if margin is too thin.

**"What if nobody wants it?"**
- Launch with one client at full price before productizing $X.
- Get feedback. Refine. Re-launch.
- Not "test" it at discount; test it at real price with one real client.

## Pricing Models

**Per-Project:** Charge one price for the entire deliverable ($2,000-5,000).

**Per-Month Retainer:** Charge recurring monthly for ongoing work ($1,000-3,000/month).

**Per-Quarter:** Charge every 3 months for periodic updates or services ($2,000-4,000/quarter).

**Hybrid:** Charge for the first project, then a lower retainer for ongoing support.

## From Productized Service to Delegation

Once your productized service is dialed in and you're consistently delivering, you can:

1. **Hire a delivery person** to execute while you sell and strategize
2. **Keep 50% of revenue** as profit after paying the delivery person 40-50%
3. **Sell more** because you're no longer bottlenecked by your own time
4. **Raise price** knowing you have someone to deliver at the lower price you initially charged

This is how you scale a service business beyond yourself.

## The Next Level

Once you master one productized service, you can:
- Add a second service (vertical integration)
- Create a digital product that complements your service
- Build recurring revenue through retainers
- Gradually transition to pure product/SaaS if desired

The beauty of a productized service is that it's a bridge. You start with your expertise and time, optimize the hell out of it, and then either scale through hiring or move into products.

Start here. Master it. Then scale.
