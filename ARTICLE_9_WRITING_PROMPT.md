# Article 9 Writing Prompt: Repurposing Content for Revenue

## Meta
- **Topic:** "One Article, Five Revenue Streams: How to Repurpose Content Into Clients"
- **Pillar:** Getting Clients
- **Category:** Marketing
- **Credibility Source:** Framework-based (proven repurposing multiplier)
- **Opening Type:** Question-First (How many customers are hidden in your best articles?)
- **Structure:** Question Intro → Problem → 5-Step Framework → Real Math → Implementation
- **Tone:** Tactical/Strategic
- **Target Audience:** Content creators (writers, consultants, solopreneurs with published articles) who want more ROI from their content
- **Word Count Target:** 3,400–4,000 words
- **Read Time Target:** 14 min

## Core Problem
You've written 20+ articles. Each gets 1–5 leads/month. Most repurposing is one-way (article → email newsletter → done). But one article can generate leads across 5+ channels if you structure it right.

The multiplier: 1 heavier article + 5 formats = 10–15x more reach = 3–5x more leads.

## Key Insights

### 1. The Silent Waste
- Content creators obsess over writing the article
- They publish it. Share once on Twitter and LinkedIn.
- The article is done. Next article.
- Actual reach: 30% of total potential

### 2. The Five Formats
- 1. LinkedIn thread (long-form social)
- 2. Twitter thread (short-form social)
- 3. Email sequence (5–7 emails mining the article)
- 4. Short-form clips (TikTok/Reels/YouTube Shorts)
- 5. Podcast outline or solo episode script

Each format reaches different people. Same core content, different channels.

### 3. The Math
- Original article: 500 readers
- +LinkedIn thread: +200 readers (new audience)
- +Email sequence: +150 (builds email list)
- +Twitter thread: +100 (reaches rephrasers/sharers)
- +Short clips: +300 (algorithmic boost)
- +Podcast: +400 (new listeners)
- **Total reach: 1,650 (vs. 500 original) = 3.3x multiplier**

### 4. The Lead Impact
- This isn't just vanity. It's lead generation.
- Each format has different conversion mechanics
- Email sequence: 2–5% convertLinkedIn thread: 0.5–1% convert (lower intent)
- Podcast: 5–10% convert (high intent)
- Your best lead channel might be the one you're ignoring

### 5. Time Investment (It's Not That Much)
- Writing the article: 4–6 hours
- Repurposing into 5 formats: 2–3 hours total
- But spread over 2–4 weeks (not all at once)
- Per-format time: 20–30 minutes each
- ROI: 5 hours repurposing → 3x lead generation = worth it

## Opening Question Hook
"How many customers are sitting in your best article, waiting to be reached on a platform where you haven't shown up yet?"

## Structure

### 1. The Hidden Opportunity (Question + Stat)
- Most solopreneurs publish but don't repurpose
- This is a lead generation leak
- The platforms where your best customers hang out are not where your article is

### 2. Why Repurposing Works (Psychology)
- People discover content differently
- Some find you via LinkedIn, some via email, some via Twitter
- One article reaches different segments via different channels
- Repetition (across platforms) actually increases conversion, not annoyance

### 3. The Five Format Framework
Each with:
- **Why this format works**
- **How long it takes**
- **Who you reach that you didn't reach before**
- **Lead quality from this format**
- **Implementation walkthrough** (real example)

#### Format 1: LinkedIn Long-Form (600–1,200 words)
- Why: LinkedIn algorithm favors native long-form; reaches professional audience
- Time: 30 minutes (edit article into LinkedIn voice)
- Who: Professional audience, decision-makers
- Lead quality: Medium (they might just save the post)
- Hook structure: "Here's what most [profession] don't know about [topic]..."

#### Format 2: Twitter/X Thread (8–12 tweets)
- Why: Reaches different timezone, rephrasing algorithms, quote-tweeters boost it
- Time: 20 minutes
- Who: Fast readers, idea-first people
- Lead quality: Low (high reach, low conversion, but can convert at scale)
- Hook: Start with most contrarian/surprising claim

#### Format 3: Email Sequence (5–7 emails)
- Why: Builds/nurtures list; highest conversion rate
- Time: 45 minutes (structure the article into email sequence)
- Who: Email subscribers who value depth, willing to read long-form
- Lead quality: High (they opted in, reading 5+ emails from you)
- Structure: Hook email → 3-4 teaching emails → CTA email → social proof email

#### Format 4: Short-Form Clips (15–60 second video/carousel)
- Why: Algorithm boost on TikTok/Reels/Shorts, reaches mobile-first audience
- Time: 40 minutes (write script, record quick clips)
- Who: Younger, mobile-first audience (even B2B)
- Lead quality: Medium
- Hook: Extract the most "wow" moment from the article

#### Format 5: Podcast Script or Solo Episode
- Why: Audio audience; you become recognizable voice; high trust/high conversion
- Time: 60 minutes (write + record OR provide to podcast guest)
- Who: Commuters, runners, chores-doers; very engaged audience
- Lead quality: Very High (they're listening to long-form, have high intent)
- Angle: Tell the story behind the article, not just teach the content

### 4. The Mechanics (Real Examples)
- Walk through 1 full article and show all 5 formats in practice
- Article 1 example (article title + how it played across channels)
- Metrics: X views via LinkedIn, Y via email, Z via Twitter, etc.

### 5. The Implementation Checklist (Week by week)
- Week 1: Publish article, plan 5 formats
- Week 2: LinkedIn thread + email sequence
- Week 3: Twitter thread + YouTube Shorts script
- Week 4: Podcast guest pitch (or solo record)
- Week 5+: Retarget non-converters via email

### 6. CTA
- Pick one article you've written
- Commit to repurposing it into 3 formats this week
- Track interest/leads from each
- Measure (which format generated the most leads?)

- **Permission:** "You don't have to be on every platform; you have to be on multiple formats of the same idea"
