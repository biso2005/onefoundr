## Opening Hook (80-120 words)
You have one offer. One price. One option.

So every customer who wants less pays the full price. Every customer who wants more is disappointed. And you're leaving money on the table because you can't charge more for deeper needs.

The solution isn't to have ten offers. It's to have three tiers that serve different customers different ways.

This isn't about nickel-and-diming clients. It's about matching price to actual value received.

## Structure Outline (4-5 main sections)

### Section 1: The One-Offer Problem
- Single offer caps your revenue: Some customers want less (price-sensitive), some want more (deeper)
- Single offer frustrates customers: Budget constraints, different needs
- Single offer looks unprofessional: Consultants and agencies use tiers; solo solopreneurs don't
- The gap: Between your "good enough" and "comprehensive" is untapped revenue

### Section 2: The 3-Tier Framework
**Tier 1: The Starter** (40% of customers, 20% of revenue)
- Entry point (lower barrier)
- Solo DIY framework or minimal coaching
- Price: 30-40% of your full offer
- Serves: Budget-conscious customers, self-directed learners
- Example: "$1,500 DIY package" vs "$5,000 full service"

**Tier 2: The Standard** (50% of customers, 50% of revenue)
- Your current offer (the bulk)
- Good balance of self-service and support
- Price: Your current rate (100%)
- Serves: Most customers (the "Goldilocks" sweet spot)

**Tier 3: The Premium** (10% of customers, 30% of revenue)
- Comprehensive, hands-on, concierge
- Extra support, faster turnaround, customization
- Price: 150-200% of tier 2
- Serves: Customers with urgent needs or premium requirements

### Section 3: How to Build Your Tiers
- Start with your current offer (Tier 2)
- Remove 40% of what you do → Tier 1 (DIY framework + light coaching)
- Add 50% more intensity → Tier 3 (white-glove service, priority support)
- Price accordingly (1/3 / 1x / 2x ratio roughly works)
- Test: Launch tiers and see which attracts which customers

### Section 4: Niche-Specific Examples
- Coaching: DIY course, standard coaching, VIP intensive
- Design: Template kit, custom design, ongoing retainer
- Copywriting: Self-service swipe file, 1-project copy, monthly copy partnership
- Each tier is building block on previous (not completely different offerings)

### Section 5: Why This Works (Psychology + Economics)
- Anchoring: Premium tier makes standard feel like good value
- Segmentation: Customers self-select by need and budget
- Revenue: You capture more (premium moves from "$0" negotiation to "$10k" tier)
- Simplicity: 3 choices is ideal (2 is limiting, 4+ is confusing)

## Key Insights to Weave In
- Tiering is not nickel-and-diming; it's service design
- Many customers would pay more for depth—you just never asked
- Tier 3 attracts premium customers (willing to pay = invested in results)
- Tiers let you serve more people without burning out
