# Guide Brief

## Topic
A practical, step-by-step guide that shows non-technical small business owners how to set up, run, and get results from email marketing without hiring an agency.

## Primary Keyword
email marketing for small businesses

## Secondary Keywords
- small business email strategy
- email campaigns for beginners
- best email marketing tools
- how to build an email list
- email marketing tips

## Target Audience
Small business owners with 1–20 employees who know they should be doing email marketing but haven't started or tried and gave up. They're not tech-savvy, operate on a limited budget, and want tactics they can implement in hours (not weeks). They care about straightforward results, not marketing theory.

## Search Intent
The reader wants to learn a clear, do-it-yourself system for email marketing that produces opens, clicks, and customers. By the end of the guide, they should be able to pick a tool, build a signup flow, launch a welcome sequence, send their first campaign, and measure the results.

## Word Count
2,500 words — long enough to include setup steps, a short case study, tested subject-line formulas, screenshots/examples, and practical templates without needless filler.

## Brand Voice
Friendly expert — knowledgeable but conversational, slightly opinionated, zero jargon; explains things like a helpful friend over coffee.

## CTA Goal
Download the free email marketing checklist (lead magnet) — converts readers into subscribers and leads for audits/services.

## Unique Angle
- [RECOMMENDED] Real results case study: show the exact sequence and metrics used to grow a client from 200 → 5,000 subscribers in 6 months (anonymized screenshots and timeline) — proves the approach actually works and gives readers a concrete blueprint.
- Contrarian tool recommendation: recommend MailerLite (practical, cheap, easy to migrate) and explain why Mailchimp and other big platforms often cost small teams money without benefit.
- Plain-text-first approach: evidence and anecdotal A/B tests showing plain-text emails often outperform heavy templates for small lists.
- Subject-line playbook: include the top 12 tested subject-line formulas and the 3 that performed best in the author's tests, with exact examples to copy.
- Two-week quick-win plan: a prescriptive, day-by-day checklist so readers can act immediately.

Competitors to Beat

   Strength: Comprehensive, well-structured beginner content and strong domain authority

2. Mailchimp resources (likely URL: https://mailchimp.com/resources/email-marketing-for-small-business/)
   Strength: Practical templates and product-oriented guidance with examples
   Gap: Content can push readers toward Mailchimp features and paid plans; less focus on low-cost alternatives and plain-text strategies

3. Neil Patel / Niche authority (likely URL: https://neilpatel.com/blog/beginners-guide-email-marketing/)
   Strength: Actionable tips and marketing funnels; strong SEO reach
   Gap: Often high-level tactics aimed at marketers, not non-technical small business owners; lacks simple step-by-step checklists and real small-business case studies
