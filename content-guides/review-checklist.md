# Final Review Checklist — Human Gut-Check (5 min)

**Purpose**: Before publishing, a human reads this checklist and makes the final call: Is this ready?

**Time**: ~5 minutes. If something feels off, dig into it.

---

## QUICK SCAN (2 min)

- [ ] **Headline hook** — Does the H1 make you want to read? Would you click this in Google search results?
- [ ] **First paragraph** — Does it sound like a real person? Or does it feel robotic/corporate?
- [ ] **Tone match** — Does it match the brief's voice description? (friendly, direct, expert, contrarian, etc.)
- [ ] **Length feel** — Does it feel complete or rushed? Too long or too short for the topic?

---

## SKIMMING CHECK (2 min)

Read these sections quickly:

- [ ] **Intro** — Hooks you immediately, promises value, feels human.
- [ ] **One random H2 section** — Specific, actionable, not fluffy.
- [ ] **Conclusion** — Motivates you to act, clear next step.
- [ ] **Unique angle** — Does the guide include the special insight/data/opinion from the brief? ✓ Yes / ❌ No

---

## GUTS CHECK (1 min)

Ask yourself:

1. **Would I send this to a friend?** If no, something's wrong. Trust that feeling.
2. **Is there anything that feels "AI-written"?** Stiff language, robotic transitions, overused words? If yes, flag it.
3. **Are opinions present?** Does the author have a personality and point of view, or is it fence-sitting? If fence-sitting, that's a problem.
4. **Does the CTA feel natural or pushy?** Should feel like a helpful suggestion, not sales-y.

---

## RED FLAGS — Stop & Fix

If ANY of these are true, send back for revision:

- ❌ **Banned AI words found** (delve, leverage, robust, seamless, cutting-edge, game-changer, etc.) — Search for them, rewrite
- ❌ **First paragraph feels robotic** — "In today's world," "Have you ever wondered," generic opening
- ❌ **No personality, no opinions** — Pure information dump with no voice
- ❌ **Vague examples** — "Use tools," "follow best practices" with no specific names or numbers
- ❌ **Conclusion just lists what was covered** — No motivation or real CTA
- ❌ **Unique angle from brief is missing** — The special insight/data/story they asked for isn't there
- ❌ **CTA doesn't match the brief goal** — Brief says "sign up for checklist," guide says "buy our course"
- ❌ **Keyword feels stuffed** — Primary keyword in sentences that sound unnatural when read aloud

---

## QUICK FIXES (If Needed)

**Robotic language?**
- Replace: "In today's digital landscape..." → "Here's the thing..."
- Replace: "Leverage your email list" → "Use your email list"
- Replace: "Robust solution" → "Works well" or "Handles the job"

**Pale personality?**
- Add 1-2 sentences of genuine opinion: "I'd skip X" or "Most people get Y wrong"
- Add 1 personal story or observation: "I tried this and..."
- Use contractions naturally throughout

**Weak CTA?**
- Make it specific: "Download the free checklist" (not "Learn more")
- Make it feel like a suggestion: "Here's the checklist we use" (not "Buy now")

---

## APPROVAL DECISION

### PASS ✅ — Ready to publish
- Sounds human
- Specific examples
- Clear opinions/personality
- CTA is natural and matches brief
- No banned AI words
- Covers the unique angle

### NEEDS MINOR FIXES ⚠️ — Send back, should be quick
- Remove 2-3 AI words
- Add personality to intro
- Clarify or strengthen CTA
- Make 1-2 vague sections specific

### NEEDS MAJOR REWRITE ❌ — Send back, substantial work needed
- Feels robotic overall
- Missing unique angle entirely
- No opinions, just generic advice
- Examples are all vague
- Keyword stuffing
- CTA mismatches brief
